My Favorite EdTech Tools

What is an EdTech tool?

EdTech tools stand for Educational Technology tools. These are online resources that are developed for use in educational settings (formal or informal) to create inclusive and engaging learning experiences. Click here to learn more about the benefits of utilizing EdTech tools in your classroom, home, or informal educational setting.

The logos below are links that will take you the homepage of each of the companies. Click and explore more about each of these useful tools!

  • GSuite

    At the museum, we use Google Suites to communicate with our homeschool families. Children in our homeschool programs come to the museum 2 days a week to engage in museum programming and experience facilitated exhibit tours. As educators, we post daily reports on GSuite complete with pictures of the day's events, as well as supplemental activities that the children can do at home.

  • SeeSaw

    Since a large majority of children had to transition to virtual school due to COVID, the museum started a program called Museum Clubhouse. Children in this program come to the museum 2, 3, or 4 days a week to complete their virtual school assignemnts, but also engage in museum programs and activities. I have been working with this program for 2 months now, and have seen numerous students using the SeeSaw app, which allows teachers to provide interactive assignments of various types (matching games, listening activities, writing exercises) to their students.

  • Trello

    Trello is not so much an educational technology tool, as it is a project management tool. We use it all the time at the museum, in order to complete projects across the entire team in an organized, timely manner. Trello is incredibly user-friendly, and allows for teams to track progress on projects and assign tasks to individual team-members.
